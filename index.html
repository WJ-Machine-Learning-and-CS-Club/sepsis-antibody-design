Thesis

This research explores the potential of computational antibody engineering to revolutionize sepsis diagnostics and immunotherapy by creating high-affinity antibodies. Tailored for specific variants of Protein A, such as P02976 from Staphylococcus aureus, these antibodies are optimized for both rapid diagnostic use and immune-targeted therapies, offering a flexible solution to address critical needs in sepsis care.

Abstract

Sepsis, which causes nearly 10 million deaths annually[10] and is responsible for one in three hospital fatalities[11], demands rapid diagnostics and effective immune responses. This study leverages the latest AI and biomolecular tools, including AlphaFold2[2] and Rosetta[5], to design high-affinity antibodies optimized for the P02976 variant of SPA, an immune-evasive protein in Staphylococcus aureus. By fine-tuning the Fab region for high specificity to this variant, the engineered antibodies serve dual purposes: enabling rapid detection through LFAs and enhancing immune response for potential vaccine applications. This work highlights antibody engineeringâ€™s role in advancing sepsis care, offering adaptable solutions for both diagnostics and immunotherapy that can be precisely tuned to different SPA variants and other Sepsis inducing bacteria.
